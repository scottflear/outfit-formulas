## THE 6 TARGET ARCHETYPES (from outfit-formulas-voice skill)

**Core struggle:** "Full closet, nothing to wear"
**Her words:** "I have nothing to wear even though my closet is stuffed." / "I try on 5 things, hate them all, go back to the same jeans."

**What she needs to hear:** You're sitting on 50+ outfits you've never seen.

**Her words:** "I haven't bought anything in years because I keep expecting I'll lose weight." / "I don't want to 'give up' by buying bigger sizes."

**Core struggle:** "Body changed overnight, old formulas stopped working"

**What she needs to hear:** Your old formulas aren't broken because you did something wrong. They're broken because they were built for a body you don't have anymore.

### 4. The Mom Ghost

### 5. The Style Relic

### 6. The Scroll Comparer
**Core struggle:** "Hours on Pinterest/Instagram but nothing works on her body"

**What she needs to hear:** You don't need more inspiration. You need formulas that work on YOUR body.

## VOICE & TONE RULES
1. Speak like a stylish friend, not a fashion consultant
2. Mirror her frustration BEFORE offering the solution — she needs to feel SEEN first
3. Never correct her. Validate, then shift.
4. Use specific, visceral details. "You've tried on 5 things, hated all of them, and gone back to the same jeans."
5. Short sentences. Fragments are fine.
6. Permission-giving over prescriptive
7. Identity language over appearance language — "feeling like herself again" not "looking hot"
8. Acknowledge the real emotions — tears, shame, avoidance, invisibility
9. Never assume she wants to lose weight
10. "You" not "women" — make it personal

## WORDS TO NEVER USE
- "Capsule wardrobe" → "Those 10 things you always reach for"
- "Curated essentials" → "Your staples" / "What actually works"
- "Decision fatigue" → "That morning where nothing works"
- "Effortless chic" → "Looking like you have your act together"
- "Elevated" → "Actually good"
- "Flattering" (careful) → "Looks good on you" / "Works for your body"
- "Slimming" → NEVER USE
- "Age-appropriate" → NEVER USE
- "Anti-aging" → NEVER USE
- "Body type" (as classification) → "Your body" / "Your shape right now"
- "Polished" → "Put-together"

## CONTENT FORMULA (Mirror → Validate → Shift → Promise → CTA)
1. MIRROR — Describe her exact experience so specifically she thinks you're reading her mind
2. VALIDATE — Tell her she's not alone and she's not broken
3. SHIFT — Reframe the problem ("You don't have a wardrobe problem. You have a visibility problem.")
4. PROMISE — What changes with the solution
5. CTA — Clear, identity-driven action
